"To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee

This classic novel is set in a small town in Alabama during the 1930s and explores themes of racism, injustice, and the loss of innocence. The story is told through the eyes of a young girl named Scout Finch, who grows up witnessing the unfair treatment of African Americans in her community. Her father, a lawyer named Atticus Finch, is assigned to defend a black man who has been falsely accused of raping a white woman. The trial exposes the deep-seated prejudices of the town and leads to tragic consequences.


984" by George Orwell

This dystopian novel is set in a future totalitarian society where individualism and independent thinking are forbidden. The story follows the life of Winston Smith, a man who works for the ruling party and begins to rebel against their oppressive regime. He falls in love with a fellow rebel named Julia and they attempt to overthrow the government together. The novel is a warning against the dangers of totalitarianism and the importance of preserving freedom and individuality.


The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald

Set in the Roaring Twenties, this novel explores the lives of the wealthy elite and their pursuit of the American Dream. The story is told through the eyes of Nick Carraway, a young man who moves to New York City and becomes involved with the glamorous and mysterious Jay Gatsby. Gatsby is obsessed with winning back his former lover, Daisy Buchanan, and throws extravagant parties in an attempt to impress her. The novel is a critique of the decadence and excess of the Jazz Age and the emptiness of the American Dream.
